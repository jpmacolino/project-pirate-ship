- `[CANON]` **Erathal** is a continent to the **north** — the homeland behind the "immigrant from Erathal" creation option (§5.12).

## Geography
*The MVP's physical stage. The slice plays out on the **north shore of a single island**; everything else here is orientation and `[V1+]`.*

### The island — Tethryn
- `[CANON]` **Tethryn** is a small offshore island off Thalvath's **north coast**, separated from the mainland by a strait. It is **elf-dominant**.
- `[CANON]` **Climate is mild** — this part of Thalvath has little true winter; even the winter months stay temperate on the coast. The Ancient Elvish month-names speak of a harder cold than the island feels (Frozga = "cold / dead"), so a newcomer from the colder north (Erathal) finds it **warm for the season**.
- `[CANON]` **North shore — the fishing camp** (the MVP curtain; §5.5, §7.4): exclusively elven, **seasonal** (tents, drying racks, beached boats), tied directly to the town. Its specific fishing season ties to the calendar `[PROVISIONAL — to pin]`.
- `[CANON]` **South shore — the town:** predominantly elven, with a **dock** just to its west; directly connected to the camp. `[V1+]` as a built place — not entered in the MVP.
- `[CANON]` **Interior — forest.** `[V1+]` content lives here; its specifics are author canon and **not recorded** in this bible.
- `[CANON]` **Offshore, to the north:** fishing boats working the local grounds.

### The MVP corridor (north shore only)
- `[CANON]` The **wreck** lies on the north / northwest shore; the player comes ashore there.
- `[CANON]` A coastal **path ("the road"; §5.4)** runs east along the north shore from the wreck to the camp. The slice is **wreck → road → camp**, and goes no further — not the town, not the interior, not the mainland.
- *Not recorded here:* the debris search and the road beats themselves — those are build (spec §5.3–5.4), not geography.

### The mainland & Freeport
- `[CANON]` Thalvath's **mainland** lies south, across the strait (mountainous and forested).
- `[CANON]` **Freeport** sits at the mainland's **north end**, just across the water south of the island town — the wrecked ship's intended destination (→ **The Frame**).
- `[V1+]` Freeport is a large, **mixed** city with **human roots** (over a third human; nearly every sentient species present). Not built for the MVP.
- `[V1+]` The town's regular boat route to Freeport, and the **strait's name**, are V1 — parked.

## Species (MVP)
*World texture for the peoples the slice involves. Mechanics — attributes, skills, traits — live in spec §5.12, not here.*

### Elves — the fishing camp
- `[CANON]` Sea-faring southern elves (the §5.12 default elf); the camp is **exclusively elven** (→ **Geography**).
- `[CANON]` A **generational maritime community**: the eldest are old enough to have **lived through the merfolk war** (~360 years past); the rest are their descendants and kin. Elves live ~1000 years, so most of the camp is **in their hundreds**, alongside a few children and a dozen-ish young adults.
- `[CANON]` **Small** — no more than a couple dozen, and with boats out on the water, fewer still are present when the player arrives. This is *why* the curtain is one reactive encounter (§5.5), not a crowd.

### Hájje
- `[CANON]` A feared people carrying an **inherited heritage stigma**. `[AUTHOR-CANON]` The full origin is held outside this bible (§5.12 guardrail) — referenced only in the abstract, never graphic.
- `[CANON]` Today's Thalvath Hájje descend from those who **fled** that legacy: insular, settled inland, peaceable, sharing the continent's live-and-let-live ethos. They **avoid the sea** — so a Hájje at a coastal fishing camp is doubly out of place.
- `[V1+]` New Hájjeona (their inland Thalvath settlement) and the original Hájjeona in Erathal are backdrop, not slice.

### Merfolk
- `[CANON]` A sea-people; **mind-magic / illusion** is their signature power and was their worst weapon in the war. Aggressors of 1289–1294, **unseen for centuries** since (→ **Factions**, **The Frame**). The frame's subject — not a species the player meets in the MVP.

### Humans
- `[CANON]` Widespread and cosmopolitan (humans have grown most over the centuries); the camp is **neutral** toward them. The "flexible" creation identity (§5.12); human-rooted Freeport is their kind of place (→ **Geography**, `[V1+]`).

## Factions & Relations
*MVP-scoped: the historical conflict that fuels the frame, and the dispositions the slice actually touches.*

### The merfolk war (1289–1294 MT)
- `[CANON]` In **1289 MT**, merfolk launched a **coordinated attack** across several coastal communities, overwhelming smaller villages and towns. **Freeport** was the one city that mustered and held.
- `[CANON]` The open invasion stalled, but **five years of strife** followed — ships under constant threat, new defenses devised against merfolk, and many people migrating inland (seeding the larger landlocked cities). A **truce** held late in **1294 MT**.
- `[CANON]` **Mind-magic / illusion was the merfolk's worst weapon** — the root of the enduring "they lure ships to their doom" fear, and what makes the frame credible (→ **The Frame**).
- `[CANON]` Since the truce, **no one has reliably seen a merfolk.** Rumored wrecks are still blamed on them, and the odd sailor's tale is waved off as a trick of the light. They are now a **vanished, half-legendary enemy** — which is exactly why an accusation can't be disproven.
- `[V1+]` The merfolk's own side of the war, their whereabouts, and their society are author canon / V1.

### Dispositions the slice touches
- `[CANON]` **The elf camp's stance** (grounds the §5.12 gate bias): an insular elven community — warm to its own (elf → favorable), wary of outsiders (human → neutral), fearful of Hájje (→ disadvantage). Sharpened by the camp's **living memory of the war** and §7.4's stranger-from-the-sea unease.
- `[CANON]` **Hájje stigma:** the bias is **unfair inherited prejudice** — fear of a heritage (→ **Species**), not of anything the living Hájje have done. Disadvantage the player is meant to feel as *unjust*.

## The Frame
*The central deception of the MVP slice: a deliberate wreck staged to look like a merfolk attack.*

### What is true
- `[CANON]` A ship **bound for the mainland** was destroyed at sea off Thalvath's **north coast** (~1650 MT), wrecking at the island of **Tethryn**, which was not its destination.
- `[CANON]` The ship's destination was **Freeport**, a city on the Thalvath mainland. `[V1+]` as a *place* (not built for MVP); recorded because the player **remembers being bound there**, which grounds "I don't belong on this island."
- `[CANON]` The cause was a **deployed magical device** — directed, destructive magic, **not** any creature. The destruction was deliberate.
- `[CANON]` The act was **staged to read as a merfolk attack**.
- `[CANON]` **Merfolk did not do this. They are being framed.**
- `[AUTHOR-CANON]` Who deployed the device, and why, is held outside this bible and is **not revealed in MVP**. Abstracted truth recorded here: *a deliberate act by a hidden party.*

### What the wreck bears (evidence, as world fact)
- `[CANON]` The **surface evidence** is consistent with a violent attack at sea; to an untrained or already-fearful observer it reads as merfolk work.
- `[CANON]` **One detail does not fit merfolk methods.** It is real and physically present whether or not anyone notices it, and it has two dimensions:
  - *Physical:* directed, forceful breakage — the mark of a made force, not the bite, ram, or crush any sea-creature leaves.
  - *Arcane:* residual **destructive** arcana — the wrong school for merfolk illusion/mind-magic — whose signature is **unfamiliar** (legibly *wrong*, not legibly *whose*).
- `[CANON]` The device left **physical fragments** — remnants of a made thing with no place in a natural wreck.
- `[CANON]` What the evidence honestly supports: **not merfolk** — and **no culprit named**. The mystery stays open.

### What is believed (and why the frame holds)
- `[CANON]` Coastal communities read the wreck as a merfolk attack.
- `[CANON]` The frame holds because of three standing pressures:
  - **Motive / bad blood** — the centuries-old merfolk–landbound war (→ **Factions**).
  - **Believable mechanism** — merfolk truly possess illusion and mind-magic, so "they lured the ship" is credible.
  - **Immediate fuel** — a recent pattern of lost ships has left coastal communities uneasy and quick to blame the sea-folk (→ **Timeline**).

### Scope notes
- `[CANON]` Merfolk are a full species and faction in their own right; their innocence here is a fact *of the frame*, not the boundary of their role (→ **Species**, **Factions**).
- *Not recorded here* (by design): the player's discovery sequence, the basic-vs-thorough search mechanic, the class-lens readings, and the "lone doubter" arc — those are design/build (spec §5.3, §5.8, §5.9), not world facts.

## Factions & Peoples
*MVP scale: the groups and history the frame and the gate turn on. Playable-race **mechanics** live in spec §5.12; this records only the **world facts** the slice and guardrails need. A standalone Species section isn't needed for MVP — §5.12 plus the abstract notes here cover it.*

### The merfolk war (1289–1294 MT)
- `[CANON]` In **1289 MT** the merfolk launched a coordinated assault across Thalvath's coastal communities, overwhelming the smaller villages and towns. **Freeport** was the one city that mustered and drove them off.
- `[CANON]` The open invasion failed, but **~five years of strife** followed — ships under constant threat, new defenses devised against merfolk, and many people migrating inland (seeding the larger landlocked cities). A **truce** held late in **1294 MT**.
- `[CANON]` Merfolk wield **illusion / mind-magic** — among their worst weapons in that war. This is the keystone the frame rests on: it makes "they lured the ship" believable rather than absurd.

### The merfolk since
- `[CANON]` Since the truce the merfolk have effectively **vanished** — unseen for ~360 years. What remains is rumor: ships occasionally "taken," sightings dismissed as a trick of the light.
- `[CANON]` They are therefore a **vanished, half-legendary enemy**, not a present population. *(Revises spec §5.8's "integrated into society.")*
- `[CANON]` Why the frame sticks here: centuries-deep fear, the ambient "they still take ships" rumor, and **no merfolk anyone can produce** as counter-evidence. The absent are the easiest to blame.
- `[AUTHOR-CANON]` The merfolk's eventual **return** is a future thread — outside the MVP, not recorded here.

### The fishing camp — the gate community
- `[CANON]` Exclusively **elven**; a small, **generational maritime community.** A couple dozen are **ashore** at a given time, with another dozen or two out on the **boats** — a full community of roughly three or four dozen.
- `[CANON]` The **eldest lived through the merfolk war**; the rest descend from them or close kin. The community **skews old** — most are centuries old (elves live ~1,000 years), with only a handful of younger elves and a few children.
- `[CANON]` **Living war-memory is the frame's local amplifier:** the camp doesn't merely inherit the old fear — its elders *survived* the merfolk. A wreck that reads as their work lands on people who remember them firsthand.
- `[CANON]` **Disposition** (grounds the §5.12 gate bias): a touch **warmer to fellow elves** as kin, **normal warmth to other outsiders** (the world's live-and-let-live norm — see Tone), and the **one real exception: fear of Hájje** (the heritage stigma). Natural caution toward an unknown shipwreck survivor is circumstantial, not bigotry. A camp this small means the gate is **one face**, not a crowd (fits §5.5; keeps the NPC/asset surface tiny).

### The Hájje — as the slice meets them
- `[CANON]` A **feared people carrying an inherited heritage stigma.** Today's Thalvath Hájje descend from those who **fled** that legacy, settled inland, kept to themselves, and took up the continent's live-and-let-live ethos. The fear they meet is **unfair inherited prejudice, not earned** — which is what makes the disadvantage land as injustice (right for the Teen tone).
- `[CANON]` **Appearance (species rule):** pale-skinned elves with **black hair and black eyes** — the visible marker that lets the camp recognize a Hájje **on sight**, so the bias can trigger diegetically. They read as visibly *elven*: to an elf camp, a dark reflection of their own kind rather than a foreign species, which deepens the unease.
- `[CANON]` **Hájje avoid the sea** — so a Hájje at an elven *fishing* camp is doubly out of place: feared heritage *and* a landlubber among sea-people. Sharpens the disadvantage demo (§5.10, §5.12).
- `[AUTHOR-CANON]` The heritage's full nature (Yezurkstal; the origin) stays **abstract and outside the agent's reach** (§5.12 content guardrail): feared origin, fled it, stigma — nothing graphic.
- `[V1+]` New Hájjeona (inland Thalvath), the original Hájjeona in Erathal, and Yezurkstal's sons are backdrop — not in the slice.

## Timeline & Calendar
*MVP-scoped: the dates that ground the frame and the peoples, plus the working calendar. Deep-past (pre-slice) chronology is author canon.*

### The calendar (current)
- `[CANON]` The standard calendar is still in use. Era marker: **MT = Millitrenous**, "the current age."
- `[CANON]` The year runs **356 days** across **12 months** and **4 seasons**, begins in **spring**, under **one moon**.
- `[CANON]` The camp is **in-season** at the north shore now — active through this late-winter / early-spring stretch. `[V1+]` Its full migratory cycle (when it arrives, when it relocates down-shore) stays flexible.

### Chronology the slice rests on
- `[CANON]` **1289 MT** — the merfolk launch the coastal war; Freeport holds.
- `[CANON]` **1294 MT** — truce; the merfolk vanish thereafter.
- `[CANON]` **1650 MT** — present; the wreck. The war sits **~360 years back**: living memory for the eldest camp elves, half-legend for everyone else.
- `[CANON]` **The slice opens in late Frozga, 1650 MT** — the last weeks of winter, a few weeks before **1 Pertga** (the new year and the turn into spring).
- `[V1+]` **~1150 MT** Hájje migrate south to Thalvath · **~1175 MT** Yezurkstal's sons fall. Backdrop for the Hájje stigma — not slice events.
- `[V1+]` **~1150–1250 MT** — a broad wave of migration south from Erathal in Yezurkstal's aftermath, people seeking a fresh start away from those memories. The Hájje migration above was one strand of it.
- `[CANON]` Erathal and Thalvath remain linked by **ongoing migration and regular sea trade** into the present — the backdrop to the "immigrant from Erathal" origin (§5.12) and the reason ships ply this route.

## Tone & Register
*How the world should feel and be rendered, plus the handling rules for loaded material. Not world facts (those live in their sections); not the full systems behind them (those are V1 / author canon).*

### Aesthetic
- `[CANON]` Vibrant, adventurous **sword-and-sorcery** on a **grand scale** — a real sense of **wonder and adventure**, and a **hopeful** outlook on the world despite its dark history. Not grimdark.

### Tech & magic register
- `[CANON]` **Age of sail.** The golden age of seafaring — frigates, brigantines, galleons. The wrecked vessel and the camp's boats belong to this world.
- `[CANON]` **Magic is commonplace yet wondrous.** Everyday magic is normalized — runestone amenities (cold-boxes, multi-burner stoves, and the like) are ordinary fixtures on ships and in homes. But *substantial* spellcraft is rare: most people wield little or none, and a powerful working (a druid raising a golem, a geomancer cracking the ground) is uncommon and awe-inspiring. *Consequence:* the wreck's destructive arcane signature is conspicuous precisely because such magic is rare.
- `[V1+]` The magic *system* behind this — latent arcane energy in the world's rocks, trees, and people; how runestones (Alset of Marftaport) spread and shifted access; the taxonomy of magic types — is post-MVP / author canon. The slice needs the register, not the system.

### Handling rules (guardrail-adjacent)
- `[CANON]` **Prejudice is unusual in this world.** The norm is live-and-let-live: even a single-species community welcomes an outside Lizock no less warmly than an outside Human. So bigotry, where it appears, is the **exception** and must be motivated.
- `[CANON]` The camp's **fear of Hájje is that exception** — rooted in the inherited heritage stigma and old war-trauma, never generic xenophobia. It must read as **felt injustice**: the world is kind, and this fear is the unfair crack in it.
- `[CANON]` The **dark heritage stays deep background** — referenced abstractly, never depicted; nothing graphic (§5.12 guardrail).

### Presentation
- `[CANON]` **Visible tabletop.** Show the dice, DCs, modifiers, and advantage/disadvantage, each with an authored label. Disadvantage especially must always read as **fair and understandable** the instant it lands (§1, §5.10).
